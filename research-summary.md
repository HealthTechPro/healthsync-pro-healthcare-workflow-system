# Research Summary — HealthSync Pro

## Core Problem
Healthcare delays in bariatric surgery and weight management are not caused by lack of clinical knowledge, but by fragmented, outdated, and inconsistent documentation across systems.

Key gaps include:
- outdated BMI and weight data
- missing lifestyle documentation
- inconsistent follow-up tracking
- lack of structured prior authorization evidence

---

## Medical Necessity Requirements (High-Level)
Most bariatric and anti-obesity medication approvals require:

- BMI ≥ 30–35 depending on criteria
- presence of comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, etc.)
- documented failed non-surgical treatments
- ongoing lifestyle modification
- evidence of clinical monitoring and follow-up

---

## Key Data Inputs

### Anthropometric (High Frequency)
- weight
- BMI
- body fat %
- waist circumference

### Behavioral & Lifestyle
- nutrition adherence
- exercise participation
- coaching visits
- activity tracking (wearables, gym)

### Clinical Data
- comorbidities
- medication history
- contraindications
- follow-up visits

---

## Derived Indicators (Critical Insight)

HealthSync Pro introduces:

- Data Recency Score  
- Stability Trend (up / down / flat)  
- Risk Flags (missing data, rapid change, inconsistencies)

These convert raw data into actionable decision support.

---

## Workflow Insight

The system shifts care from:

Reactive → Proactive  
Fragmented → Structured  
Manual → Auditable  

---

## Multidisciplinary Care Model

Effective weight management requires coordination across:

- primary care
- bariatric surgery
- endocrinology
- orthopedics
- behavioral health
- exercise physiology

This aligns with the Chronic Care Model (CCM) and Exercise is Medicine®.

---

## Payer & Prior Authorization Insight

Prior authorization requires:
- complete documentation
- recent data (often within 30–90 days)
- proof of adherence and progress

Incomplete documentation leads to:
- denials
- rework
- delays

---

## Key Innovation

HealthSync Pro addresses this by:

- standardizing data inputs
- increasing data frequency (daily → weekly)
- structuring outputs for payer readiness
- creating an auditable workflow

---

## System-Level Impact

- reduced prior authorization rework
- faster clinical decision-making
- improved patient adherence
- stronger care coordination
